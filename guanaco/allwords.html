Full Word List


Baboso - Goofy, useless, silly. Used to refer to someone irresponsible, carefree, and not serious. (see: Bayunco)

Bayunco - Goofy, useless, silly. Used to refer to someone irresponsible, carefree, and not serious. (see: Baboso)

Bicho - Boy, kid. Used to refer to young people in general. (see: Cipote)

Buso - Smart, conniving, creative. Short for abusivo, as in someone who knows how to abuse the rules or system.

Cabal - Exactly. Used to show agreement with a statement or confirm that something is correct.

Casaca - Lies. Originally meaning "straw" but has come to mean lies. (see: Paja)

Chafa - Low quality, crappy. Used to describe things, places, experiences, or really anything that is not well made or that is essentially garbage.

Chero - Friend, buddy. Used to refer to close friends.

Chivo - Cool. Used more often than "genial".

Chucho - Dog. Sometimes used in conjunction with the word "callejero" or "aguacatero" to refer to street dogs/stray dogs.

Chuco - Dirty, filthy. Used to describe anything that is dirty, literally or metaphorically.

Chunche - Thing, whatchamacallit. A filler word that can mean anything. (see: Volado)

Cipote - Boy, kid. Used to refer to young people in general. (see: Bicho)

De Choto - Used to describe anything that is free.

Pachanga - Party, shindig. Used to refer to an informal get-together, usually in the street and with loud music.

Paja - Lies. Originally meaning "straw" but has come to mean lies. (see: Casaca)

Pisto - Money. Used informally to mean money, wealth, or cash.

Puchica - Dang, sheesh. Used to express awe at a situation or event.

Puya - Dang, sheesh. Used to express awe at a situation or event. Euphamism for other spanish cuss word.

Salu - Goodbye. Informal way to say goodbye, used more frequently than "adios".

Seco - Skinny, thin. Used affectionately to refer to a skinny person.

Volado - Thing, whatchamacallit. A filler word that can mean anything. (see: Chunche)

Yinas - Slides, flip-flops, rubber shoes. Used to refer to any casual shoes, usually either open toed or made of rubber.

Yuca - Difficult, hard, tough. Gets its meaning from the Yuca, a tough root which is often fried and eaten with a red sauce.
